Syllabus for TNUSRB - Police Constable (PC) ▼

WRITTEN EXAMINATION PATTERN:

PART I. TAMIL LANGUAGE ELIGIBILITY TEST

PART II. MAIN WRITTEN EXAMINATION

The Written Examination for Sub-Inspectors of Police selection will be of degree level with basic knowledge of Class XII of all the subjects as the datum. The syllabus includes the following topics.

Part- I

1. GENERAL KNOWLEDGE – BOTH FOR OPEN AND DEPARTMENTAL CANDIDATES

General Science: It will cover general appreciation and understanding of science including matters of everyday observations and experience. Topics include:

- Scientific Instruments, Inventions and Discoveries.

- Scientists and their contributions.

- Human Physiology, Diseases and their causes, cure and prevention.

- Diet and Balanced diet, Human Genetics.

- Animals, Mammals and Birds.

- Environment and Ecology.

- Elements and Compounds, Acids, Bases and Salts.

- Motion, Newton’s Laws of motion, Properties of Matter.

- Electricity, National Laboratories and related topics.

2. HISTORY OF INDIA

- Dates and events from Indus Valley Civilization to present-day Indian modern administration.

3. GEOGRAPHY

- Regions of India, Weather, Monsoon, Climate.

- Natural Calamities, Crops, Crops pattern.

- Indian Towns, Hill Stations, National Parks.

- Major Ports, Minerals, Major Industries.

- Forests and Wildlife, Population distribution.

4. ECONOMICS AND COMMERCE

- Agriculture, Major crops and crop pattern in India.

- Industrial Development, Major, Medium, Small Scale and Cottage Industries.

- Village and Rural Development, Housing, Drinking water schemes.

- Price policy, Inflation, Population and Unemployment problems.

5. INDIAN POLITY

- Indian Constitution, Salient Features, Citizenship.

- Elections, Parliament, State legislature, Executive.

- Judiciary system, Local Self-Governments.

- Centre-State Relations, Language Policy, Foreign Policy.

6. CURRENT EVENTS

- Latest developments in Science, Technology, Politics.

- New developments in Trade, Transport, Communication.

- Historical events, Fine Arts, Literary Works.

- Games and Sports, National, International awards.

- India and its Neighbours, Present-day India.

TECHNICAL – BOTH FOR OPEN AND DEPARTMENTAL CANDIDATES

7. ELECTRONIC DEVICES AND CIRCUITS

- Semiconductor, PN junction diode, Zener diode.

- Rectifier, Bipolar Junction Transistor, Oscillators.

- Field Effect Transistor, Unijunction Transistor.

- SCR, DIAC, TRIAC, MOSFET, Opto Electronic Devices.

- Feedback amplifier, Oscillator, IGBT, Inverters.

8. ELECTRICAL CIRCUITS AND MACHINES

- D.C. Circuits, Power, Resistance, Conductance.

- Ohm’s Law, Kirchhoff’s Law, A.C. Circuits, Resonance.

- Transformers, Power supplies, Test Instruments.

- D.C. Machines, Induction Motor, Stepper Motor.

9. MICRO ELECTRONICS

- Number System, Boolean Algebra, Logic Gates.

- Digital Logic Families, Combinational Circuits.

- Operational Amplifier and its applications.

- Digital to Analog Converter, Analog to Digital Converter.

10. MICROPROCESSOR AND ITS APPLICATION

- Introduction to Microprocessors, Microcontroller.

- 8085 Microprocessor, 8051 Microcontroller.

- Interrupt and Serial Communication for Data Transfer.

- Interfacing Techniques and Applications.

11. NETWORK, ANTENNA AND PROPAGATION

- Networks, Equalizer, Attenuator, Filters.

- Antennas: Radiation, Parameters, Oscillating dipole.

- Propagation of Radio Waves.

12. COMMUNICATION ENGINEERING AND SYSTEM

- Amplitude Modulation, Angle Modulation.

- Digital Modulation Techniques, Optical Communication.

- Wireless Communication (2G, 3G, 4G).

- Cellular Concepts, Multiple Access Techniques.

13. PROGRAMMING AND APPLICATION OF COMPUTERS

- Introduction to Computers, Hardware, Software.

- Firmware Upgrading, Troubleshooting, Scanners, Printers.

- Working with Word Processing, Spreadsheet, Database.

- Cyber Security, Cryptography, Encryption Algorithms.

14. PROGRAMMING IN “C”

- Program Development, Introduction to C.

- C Operators, I/O Statements, Decision Making.

- Simple C Programming.
